About the Club and its History

Nailsea Computer Club meets in Nailsea on Friday evenings, and has a membership from the wider North Somerset and Bristol area.

It is open to everyone and provides an opportunity to share computer knowledge and skills, learning from other members and solving problems together.

The club has been running since 1986, when it was called the Byte Home Computer Club (BHCC) and used to meet in Youth House in Nailsea. It had a membership of about sixty people with an interest in the various home/gaming computers such as the; Commodore 64, Amiga the Spectrum ZX and the Sinclair that were popular at the time.

As technology developed, the focus changed to the PC and laptops more recently broadening to include Smart phones, tablets and other devices.

Currently there are members with an interest in the various versions of Windows, Linux, Android and Apple devices.

Come along and share an interest, bring a problem and enjoy the friendly company.

The club every friday evening between 7:00pm - 10:00pm and costs £1.00 to attend for an evening or £10 for a calendar year's membership.
